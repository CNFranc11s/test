What is Privacy Prism?

Privacy Prism is an interactive analysis platform centered on the topic of data privacy. It is designed to help users understand the hidden privacy risks embedded in today's digital environment. When we post, repost, or comment on social media, short-video apps, or online platforms, we often—sometimes unknowingly reveal personal information, preferences, or behavioral patterns. These traces can be recorded, inferred, amplified, or repurposed by platform algorithms.

By entering a piece of text or a link, Privacy Prism analyzes the content through six dimensions—Exposure, Inference, Audience & Consequences, Platforms & Rules, Amplification, and Manipulability and generates a structured Privacy Risk Report.

Built on a multi-agent architecture, the system simulates different perspectives on privacy, offering a window into how algorithms interpret and process the same text from multiple angles. The goal of this platform is to make data privacy visible, tangible, and comprehensible.

In an era where algorithms shape information flows, data has become a new form of social currency: our actions, interests, and expressions are constantly collected, computed, and reassembled informing content distribution, targeted advertising, and even digital profiling. Privacy Prism aims to provide users with a renewed understanding of "privacy" in this complex landscape, helping individuals regain awareness and agency over their digital footprints.

Six Dimensions of Privacy Risk

01. EXPOSURE

Contains explicit personal details like names, phone numbers, addresses, or identifiable locations that directly reveal identity.

02. INFERENCE

Subtle cues (tone, interests, timing, or location hints) may allow algorithms to infer hidden attributes like emotions, income, or relationships.

03. AUDIENCE & CONSEQUENCES

Content may reach unintended audiences such as employers or institutions, causing misunderstandings or reputational effects.

04. PLATFORMS & RULES

Algorithms and policies of platforms shape who can see content, determining privacy exposure and circulation limits.

05. AMPLIFICATION

Platform algorithms might amplify content visibility, causing it to spread more widely and faster than expected.

06. MANIPULABILITY

Digital content can be reframed, edited, or recombined with other data, creating distortion and future misuse risks.
